5 Simple Exercises to Relieve Back Pain at Home

Back pain is one of the most common health complaints worldwide, affecting 8 out of 10 people at some point in their lives. While severe cases require professional treatment, many people can find significant relief through simple, targeted exercises performed at home.

As a physiotherapist with over a decade of experience, I've compiled the most effective exercises that my patients have found helpful. These exercises are safe for most adults, but always consult your healthcare provider before starting any new exercise routine.

1. Cat-Cow Stretch

This gentle yoga-inspired movement helps warm up the spine and relieve tension in the lower back and neck.

  • Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position
  • Cat: Round your back toward the ceiling, tucking your chin to chest
  • Cow: Drop your belly toward the floor, lifting your head and tailbone
  • Alternate between these positions for 10-15 repetitions
  • Move slowly and breathe deeply throughout

2. Knee-to-Chest Stretch

This stretch helps elongate the lower back muscles and relieve pressure on spinal nerves.

  • Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat on the floor
  • Bring one knee toward your chest, grasping behind the thigh
  • Hold for 20-30 seconds, feeling a gentle stretch in your lower back
  • Switch legs and repeat 3 times each side

3. Bird Dog Exercise

This core stabilization exercise strengthens the back muscles and improves balance.

  • Start on hands and knees in tabletop position
  • Extend your right arm forward and left leg backward simultaneously
  • Hold for 5 seconds, keeping your core engaged and back flat
  • Return to start and switch sides
  • Perform 10 repetitions on each side
"Consistency is key — doing these exercises for just 15 minutes daily can make a remarkable difference in your back pain within 2-3 weeks." — Dr. Radhe Patel

4. Pelvic Tilt

This subtle but effective exercise strengthens the abdominal muscles that support your lower back.

  • Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat on the floor
  • Tighten your abdominal muscles, pressing your lower back into the floor
  • Hold for 10 seconds, then relax
  • Repeat 15-20 times

5. Child's Pose

A restful stretch that gently elongates the entire spine and relieves tension.

  • Kneel on the floor, sitting back on your heels
  • Fold forward, extending your arms in front of you on the floor
  • Rest your forehead on the ground
  • Hold for 30-60 seconds, breathing deeply

When to See a Professional

While these exercises are beneficial for general back pain, you should seek professional help if you experience:

  • Pain that persists for more than 2 weeks despite home treatment
  • Numbness or tingling in your legs
  • Severe pain that doesn't improve with rest
  • Pain following an injury or accident
  • Difficulty with bladder or bowel control
